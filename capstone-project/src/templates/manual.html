Understanding the Multi-Channel Marketing Attribution Model

Welcome to the Multi-Channel Marketing Attribution Model dashboard. This application is a dual-tiered, real-time Machine Learning platform designed to solve one of the most complex problems in modern business: Marketing Attribution & Conversion Prediction.

Glossary

What is a Conversion?

Throughout this application, you will frequently see the terms "Converted" and "Not Converted". These are the absolute core binary outcomes of our Machine Learning models.

Converted Not Converted
  • Converted (Green): The user journey successfully resulted in the desired business objective. Depending on your industry, this could mean they purchased a product, signed a lease, or booked a B2B demo.
  • Not Converted (Red): The user journey failed or stalled. The user dropped out of the marketing pipeline before completing a transaction or signing up.

How is the "Converted" Status Determined?

Behind the scenes, the dashboard is running a highly advanced algorithm known as an XGBoost (Extreme Gradient Boosting) Classifier. Here is exactly how it decides the final outcome:

  1. Historical Training: First, the algorithm ingested thousands of past historic customer journeys. It memorized strict statistical patterns (e.g., "Users who see 8 touchpoints and start with Search Ads typically convert 92% of the time, while users who see 2 touchpoints and start with Display Ads convert only 5% of the time.").
  2. Mathematical Scoring: When you drag sliders on the dashboard, the Engine processes your specific multi-channel sequence and assigns a mathematical Probability Float from 0% to 100% based strictly on those learned historical weights.
  3. The Decision Threshold: Our engine uses a standard 50% (0.50) Confidence Threshold. If the engine calculates that your simulated journey has a probability score of 50.1% or higher, the system officially classifies that user as "Converted". If it falls to 49.9% or below, the system decides not to trigger the business objective and the user is classified as "Not Converted".
Component Breakdown

The Dashboard: Element by Element

1. Simulation Controls (The Sliders)

Located on the left side of the dashboard. Instead of static forms, we use interactive sliders. Every time you drag a slider, it instantly triggers the ML model in the background. Note: There is no "Submit" button because the entire app runs completely live.

2. The Live Inference Gauge (The Circle & Ring)

Located in the dead center of the dashboard. This is the heart of the engine.

  • The Outer Spinning Ring: Represents the continuous, real-time calculation process of the XGBoost algorithm actively monitoring your slider inputs.
  • The Percentage Number: The exact mathematical probability (from 0% to 100%) that the specific user journey you crafted will result in a Conversion.
  • The Inner Circle Color: The gauge will glow intense Green if the probability crosses the critical threshold (>50%), stamping the user as "Converted". It will glow Red if the probability is low.

3. Feature Importance (The Chart)

Located on the right side of the dashboard. Black-box Machine Learning models can be confusing. This chart creates transparency by showing you exactly why the model acts the way it does. The longer the bar next to a feature, the more overall influence that specific metric has on forcing a "Converted" status.

4. Strategic Budget Allocator

Located at the very bottom. While the tools above predict individual user outcomes (Micro-Level), this tool solves the Macro-Level business issue: "Where do we spend our money?"

$ 500,000

By typing a monetary budget into this box, the algorithm reads the Markov Chain model we processed behind the scenes. It takes your $500,000 and calculates exactly how many dollars should go to Email Marketing, Social Media, and Search Ads proportionally based strictly on their actual mathematical ability to drive revenue.
